How PulseAudit works
The buckets
Convert Candidate

The measure formula contains an EXCLUDE LOD expression — the old snapshot workaround. This metric should be switched to the native Temporality setting.

Already Converted

Temporality is already set to "Latest point in time." No action needed.

Not Applicable

No EXCLUDE LOD detected. Likely a flow or rate metric — sum behavior is correct.

Reading the table

Click any row to expand it. The expand panel shows the classification reasoning and the measure formula so you can confirm the EXCLUDE pattern before converting.

Use the bucket pills at the top to filter to just the rows you care about. The search bar filters by metric name.

The Open in Pulse ↗ link in the expand panel takes you directly to the metric in Tableau Cloud.

Converting a metric

Click Convert on any Convert Candidate row. A confirmation modal explains what will change before anything is written. Review it, then click Confirm.

After converting, the row moves to Already Converted and the bucket counts update inline — no page reload.

What subscribers see

There is no notification sent to followers when a metric's Temporality changes. The only visible signal is a "Latest Point in Time" badge that appears on the metric card.

Period-over-period comparisons and top-contributor insights will change behavior — they now compare end-of-period states instead of sums. If you're converting a high-visibility metric, let your followers know.

Reverting

Conversion is reversible. Expand any Already Converted row and click Revert to Over time to switch the metric back. The same confirmation flow applies.

Export

Export CSV in the top bar downloads the full audit results — metric name, datasource, measure field, formula, bucket, and current Temporality — for sharing or offline review.
